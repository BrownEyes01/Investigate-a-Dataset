Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

In this project, we would be investigating the No-Show Apointment dataset which consist of about 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. Details of the dataset:
Consist of 14 columns and 110527 rows. And the columns are made up of int, string and float datatypes.
Properties of the columns are:
PatientId
AppointmentID
Gender
ScheduledDay - Day someone called or registered an appointment
AppointmentDay - The day of the actual appointment, when they have to visit the doctor.
Age
Scholarship - indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program
Neighbourhood - Where the appointment takes place.
Hipertension
Diabetes
Alcoholism
Handcap
SMS_received
No-show - says ‘No’ if the patient showed up to their appointment, and ‘Yes’ if they did not show up.

Question(s) for Analysis¶


1. What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?

Conclusions¶

  1. Majority of patients tend to show up for their appointments.
  2. Females in general go for check-ups more than males.
  3. Sending a reminder SMS to the patient does not guarantee them showing up.
  4. Being enrolled into the Brazillian welfare program does not also determine if a patient shows up for an appointment.
  5. Hypertensive patients do not show up so much for appointments unlike non-hypertensive ones.
  6. Most patients show up on days they have to visit the doctor than days which they called to register the appointment.
  7. Jardim Camburi center has the highest frequency for locations for doctor's appointment but most patients tend to show up at Bonfim.

Limitations¶

  1. Not enough details on a type of sickness or factors that could actually prompt a patient to show up for an appointment.
  2. I believe not having the patients address also gave a vague analysis. We could have been able to determine why patients showed up more at Bonfim, could be due to close proximity to their location?
